What you're looking at

One hero card at the top, one alert line, one action grid. The whole screen has three regions instead of eleven. The hero is the only thing that competes for attention and it adapts to the situation:

  • Day of gig — biggest, brightest. Pulsing green badge, countdown to load-in, set times, three action chips (Navigate, Setlist, Call MD). This is the show-day mode that defines what TMG is on the day that matters.
  • In 3 days — compact next-gig card, fee visible, dress code line, taps through to gig detail.
  • Busy week — 7-day strip at the top of the hero plus the next gig prominent. Glanceable.
  • Quiet day — dashed border encouragement card. Single CTA: log your next gig. No clutter, no guilt.

The Needs you strip is the only place alerts live. Count chips, tap to drill. Hidden entirely on a quiet day. Compresses what was four separate banners (overdue, draft, offers, messages, dep request) into one row that doesn't shout.

The action grid turns Home into a launchpad. Six tiles, one tap to your most-used flows. The first tile (+ Gig) is accented because logging a gig should always be the fastest action in the app.

What's gone from this view: the 12-month forecast chart (lives in Finance now), the tax-year/this-month numeric pair (also Finance), the missing-fee wizard (rolled into the Needs you chips as 3 need fees when present), the always-on 7-day strip (now only appears in the Busy hero where it earns its space).

Decisions locked in

  • Day-of trio: Navigate / Setlist / Gig leader. Phone icon implies contact, label is the role. Avoids the MD jargon while leaving room for any band-leader / fixer / booker concept.
  • Busy week: 7-day strip on top + compact next-gig card below.
  • Action grid tile #6: "More" kebab → opens a sheet with Repertoire, Network, EPK, Profile, Settings.
  • Quiet day copy: Functional and matter-of-fact.

Schema work this unlocks

The Gig leader button needs real data to call/email. Today gigs.day_of_contact is a free-text column used inconsistently. Promoting it to a small structured shape { name, phone, email, role } unlocks:

  • One-tap call/text from the day-of hero.
  • Morning-of band confirmation SMS reminder ("Confirming I'm on for tonight's gig, 8pm").
  • Post-gig thank-you template with the right name pre-filled.

Small server change, big UX payoff. Worth roadmapping alongside the Home v2 rollout.
